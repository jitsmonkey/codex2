01
Leak #1 - Ad Creative
No Proven Bread-and-Butter Format Running
FIXABLE
The Problem

There is a format that outperforms 90% of other creatives, 90% of the time, for 90% of offers. It is simple: call out the avatar, speak to what they want, deliver the outcome, and tell them to click. None of the current ads follow this formula. The creative is skipping straight to the mechanism without first establishing who it's for and what they get, which means cold traffic scrolls past before the hook lands.

The Fix (Copy/Paste)
Paste this as your primary ad script structure. Fill in the brackets with your specific numbers and mechanism language.
copy / paste - ad creative framework 
If you own a short-term rental and want to unlock 30-50% more revenue in the next 30 days...

Here's how to copy the exact system used by the top 1% of STR hosts to fill their calendar at full nightly rates - without dropping price, running promotions, or spending hours on Airbnb research.

Watch this. [CTA]
Why this matters: Cold traffic needs to see themselves in the first sentence before they'll watch anything - this format does that in 2 seconds and then delivers the outcome, which drives click-through.
02
Leak #2 - Ad Hook Delivery
Flat Delivery Kills the Hook Before It Lands
FIXABLE
The Problem

I THINK this ad is AI generated. It's good if it is. That being said, the intonation on the hook is kinda weird, and even though the ad content is strong, flat delivery kills it. The current hook reads as a low-energy command with no emphasis or intonation on the key words. "If you own a short-term rental, never stress about filling your calendar again, without xyz, xyz, etc..." - there's no weight placed on "never stress" or "filling your calendar." The message is good. The delivery makes it sound like a Terms and Conditions read-aloud, which causes viewers to scroll past in the first 2 seconds.

The Fix (Copy/Paste)
Reshoot the hook with this script. The slash marks indicate natural pauses. The CAPS indicate where to put emphasis and raise energy. This is not shouting - it's conversational energy with intention.
copy / paste - hook delivery script with markers 
If you own a short-term rental / and you're TIRED of watching your calendar sit empty / while other hosts in your market are FULLY BOOKED...

This is going to be the most important 8 minutes you spend on your STR business this year.

Here's why. [pause - look at camera] I'm going to show you EXACTLY how to copy the system the top 1% of hosts use / to stay booked at FULL nightly rates / even in slow season.
Why this matters: Pattern interrupt and vocal energy in the first 3 seconds is the number one driver of 3-second hook rate in video ads - every extra second of watch time compounds down the funnel.
03
Leak #3 - VSL Opening
"I'll Walk You Through" Puts Viewers in Student Mode
FIXABLE
The Problem

The VSL opens with "I'll walk you through the strategy" - which immediately frames the viewer as a student who has to learn and then apply information. This creates mental distance: first I watch, then I understand, then I try. That's two extra mental steps between watching and booking. The highest-converting VSLs collapse that gap by framing the watch itself as the copy action. "You're going to learn exactly how to copy" puts them in the execution mindset from second one.

The Fix (Copy/Paste)
Replace the current VSL opening with this. It reframes watching as copying rather than learning, which puts the viewer one mental step closer to booking the call.
copy / paste - VSL opening reframe 
In the next 8 minutes, you're going to see exactly how to copy the system I use - and that I've now installed for dozens of STR hosts - to get to 90%+ occupancy at full nightly rates.

Not learn about it. Not think about whether it could work for you. Copy it.

Because by the time this video ends, you're going to know precisely what needs to change in your listing - and you'll have a clear next step to get it done.
Why this matters: Framing shifts viewer identity from passive learner to active implementer, which increases commitment to watching through to the CTA moment.
04
Leak #4 - Landing Page Hero Copy
Prehead and Subhead Too Vague to Self-Qualify Cold Traffic
FIXABLE
The Problem

The current prehead "Hosts and Portfolio Owners" identifies the audience but says nothing about what they want or the pain they're in. Cold traffic arriving from an ad about revenue growth needs to instantly feel "yes, this is for me and my exact problem." The subhead "without the guesswork, burnout or gimmicks" lists vague negatives that no STR host actually thinks in - they don't walk around saying "I have too much guesswork." They say "I don't know why my calendar is empty when the guy down the street is booked solid."

The Fix (Copy/Paste)
Replace both the prehead and subhead with these. Each one is written in the exact language your prospect uses internally when they're frustrated.
copy / paste - new prehead 
STR Hosts & Portfolio Owners Who Are Tired of Watching Their Calendar Sit Empty While Other Listings Stay Fully Booked...
copy / paste - new subhead 
Without dropping your nightly rate, paying a traditional property manager, or spending another hour trying to reverse-engineer what's working on Airbnb right now.
Why this matters: When a cold visitor reads their own internal monologue in your copy, bounce rate drops immediately because they know within 3 seconds the page was built for them.
05
Leak #5 - CTA Placement
Booking Button Visible Before VSL Earns It
FIXABLE
The Problem

The "Book Your Free Strategy Call" button is visible immediately when the page loads and links directly to Calendly. Cold traffic that hasn't watched the VSL is clicking it, booking time, and then not showing up because they were never pre-sold. This drives your no-show rate up and your show rate down. The button appearing before the VSL has done its job is like asking someone to marry you on the first date - technically they can say yes, but the conversion quality is terrible.

The Fix (Copy/Paste)
Implement a watch-time gate using Wistia's JavaScript API. Hide the real CTA and replace it with a placeholder. Reveal the CTA only after 50% watch time. Here is the complete implementation code.
copy / paste - watch gate HTML placeholder (replace your current CTA button) 
<div id="cta-locked" style="text-align:center; padding: 24px; background: #f4f4f4; border-radius: 8px; border: 2px dashed #0F0C09;">
  <p style="font-size: 16px; color: #555; margin-bottom: 0;">
    🔒   Your next step unlocks at 50% - keep watching.
  </p>
</div>

<div id="cta-unlocked" style="display:none; text-align:center;">
  <a href="https://YOUR-CALENDLY-LINK" class="cta-button">
    Book Your Free Strategy Call →
  </a>
</div>
copy / paste - Wistia watch gate JavaScript (add before closing body tag) 
<script>
window._wq = window._wq || [];
_wq.push({ id: "YOUR_WISTIA_VIDEO_ID", onReady: function(video) {
  video.bind("percentwatchedchanged", function(percent, lastPercent) {
    if (percent >= 0.50) {
      document.getElementById("cta-locked").style.display = "none";
      document.getElementById("cta-unlocked").style.display = "block";
      return video.unbind;
    }
  });
}});
</script>
Why this matters: Visitors who reach 50% watch time are pre-sold. They book with intent, they show up, and they close at a higher rate - this single change typically lifts show rate by 15-20 percentage points.
06
Leak #6 - Video Player
Free Wistia Player Creates Exit Points and Reveals Video Length
FIXABLE
The Problem

The free Wistia tier exposes the Wistia branding and "Watch on Wistia" link, giving visitors a visible exit path away from your page mid-watch. It also shows the progress bar, which immediately reveals the 8-minute video length to the viewer before they've committed to watching. Seeing "8:00" before they start is a conversion killer - most cold visitors will decide not to invest 8 minutes on something they've never heard of. The visible timeline also allows scrubbing, which lets impatient viewers skip your entire sales arc.

The Fix (Copy/Paste)
Upgrade to Wistia Pro (or Vimeo Advanced) and apply this exact settings checklist. All of these are toggle switches in the Wistia player settings panel.
copy / paste - Wistia Pro player settings checklist 
WISTIA PLAYER SETTINGS - apply all of these:

[ ] Branding: OFF - disable "Watch on Wistia" link
[ ] Player bar: HIDDEN - do not show progress bar or timeline
[ ] Playbar: DISABLE scrubbing (viewer cannot drag to skip)
[ ] Control bar: set to auto-hide after 2 seconds
[ ] Big play button: ON - clean centered play button only
[ ] End screen: set to loop or show custom end CTA
[ ] Email capture: OFF (do not gate the video)
[ ] Captions: ON with auto-generated captions enabled

EMBED CODE - use the "Inline Embed" option, not the popover.
Add &videoFoam=true to your embed URL for responsive sizing.
Why this matters: Removing the progress bar eliminates length anxiety before it starts, and removing exit links keeps 100% of your traffic on page - both directly increase watch-through rate and booked call rate.

14-day execution sprint

D1
Baseline + Tracking Cleanup

D2–3
Leak #6 - Upgrade Video Player
Upgrade Wistia to Pro (or switch to Vimeo Advanced). Apply all settings from the checklist: hide progress bar, disable scrubbing, remove Wistia branding and exit links, enable captions. Re-embed on landing page and confirm the new player loads correctly on mobile.
D4–5
Leak #5 - Install CTA Watch Gate
Replace the always-visible Calendly CTA with the locked placeholder. Paste in the Wistia JavaScript watch gate set to 50% reveal. Test the trigger on desktop and mobile. Confirm the real CTA button reveals correctly at the 50% mark before going live.
D6–7
Leak #4 - Deploy New Hero Copy
Swap in the new prehead and subhead copy on the landing page. Confirm headline, prehead, and subhead all render correctly above the fold on mobile. This is a direct copy-paste - no design changes required.
D8–9
Leak #3 - Rerecord VSL Opening
Rerecord just the first 60-90 seconds of the VSL using the new outcome-direct opening. Splice it onto the existing VSL at the edit point - no need to reshoot the full 8 minutes. Upload the updated video to Wistia and replace the embed.
D10–11
Leaks #1 + #2 - Reshoot Ad Creative
Record a new primary ad using the avatar-callout framework with the delivery markers applied. Aim for 45-60 seconds. Launch as a new ad set alongside the existing creative and let spend split 50/50 for 3 days to confirm hook rate improvement before pausing the old version.
D12–14
QA + Testing Loop
Check Wistia dashboard for updated play rate and watch-through %. Check Calendly for booked call rate change. Compare show rate on calls booked after the watch gate went live vs. before. Use these numbers as your new baseline before scaling ad spend.
