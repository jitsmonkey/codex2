What Ivy League Schools Really Look For

Elite colleges don’t want well-rounded students.

They want a well-rounded class. That means they’re looking for spikes.

Admissions officers want a clear story, a compelling narrative, and a student who brings something unique to the table.

 

But a unique story is not enough.

Students need to support that narrative with unique, meaningful extracurricular activities, get regional and national awards, and SHOWCASE that depth in the application and personal statements.
